What We Measure

Six vibe dimensions

Each neighborhood is scored across six aspects of daily life, based on point-of-interest data from OpenStreetMap. The scores reflect what's physically there — not opinions, ratings, or proprietary data.

🍽️ Food & Drink 🎭 Culture 🌙 Nightlife 🌳 Green Space 🚇 Transit 🛍️ Commerce

We do not measure or infer crime rates, income levels, property prices, or any demographic characteristics. Scores reflect POI density relative to other areas — not absolute quality judgments.

FAQ

Common questions

Does it work without an internet connection?

Yes — after installation the analysis engine runs fully offline. Map tiles need an internet connection to display, but all neighborhood data, scoring, and search runs locally. Nothing is sent to any server.

Which cities and countries are included?

The dataset covers 68,000+ cities across 200+ countries, sourced from GeoNames and OpenStreetMap. Coverage is strongest where OSM contributors have mapped points of interest thoroughly — generally urban areas in Europe, East Asia, and the Americas. Areas with sparse OSM data will have fewer neighborhoods in the results.

How is the data collected and how often is it updated?

Point-of-interest data comes from OpenStreetMap. City and country metadata comes from GeoNames. No proprietary, government, or commercial data sources are used. The dataset is rebuilt periodically as we improve the pipeline.

How does the Pro license work? Do I need an account?

No account required. After purchase via Polar, you receive a license key by email. Paste it into Settings → License in the app. One key activates one machine at a time; you can deactivate it to move to another device.

Is macOS supported?

Windows 10 and 11 are supported. macOS support is something we're working toward — it depends on testing and build stability. If you're on macOS and want to be notified, send us an email.

What does the hexagon grid represent?

The city is divided into a uniform grid of hexagonal cells using the H3 system (resolution 8 — roughly 0.7 km² per cell). Each hexagon is scored based on what points of interest exist within and nearby it. The grid makes it easier to compare adjacent areas fairly, since each cell covers the same geographic area.

What happens to my data if I cancel Pro?

Your bookmarks, presets, and local data are stored only on your device — they're never uploaded. Cancelling downgrades to Free after the billing period ends. Your local data stays intact.
